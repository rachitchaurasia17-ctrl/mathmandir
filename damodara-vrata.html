Kartik · October–November

Damodara Vrata

दामोदर व्रत — कार्तिक मास का पावन व्रत

The holy month of offering lamps to Lord Damodara

The Story Behind the Festival

The month of Kartik is dedicated to the worship of Lord Krishna in His form as Damodara — the mischievous child who was bound by the rope of His mother's love. The central pastime of this month is described in the Srimad Bhagavatam: mother Yashoda, finding baby Krishna stealing butter, attempted to bind Him to a wooden grinding mortar.

However, no matter how many ropes she tied together, the rope was always two fingers too short. Only when she became exhausted and Krishna saw her pure, unconditional love did He agree to be bound. "Dama" means rope and "udara" means belly; hence the name Damodara.

This pastime demonstrates that the Supreme Lord, who cannot be bound by the greatest mystics or yogis, is willingly conquered by the pure love of His devotee.

"To the Supreme Lord, whose form is the embodiment of eternal existence, knowledge, and bliss, whose shark-shaped earrings are swinging to and fro, who is beautifully shining in the divine realm of Gokula... I offer my humble obeisances unto Him, Lord Damodara." — Sri Damodarashtakam

Common Practices & Traditions

  • Offering Ghee Lamps (Deep Daan) — Every evening, devotees offer a lamp with ghee or sesame oil to the deity of Lord Damodara.
  • Singing the Damodarashtakam — A beautiful eight-verse prayer composed by Satyavrata Muni, glorifying the Damodara form of the Lord, is sung daily.
  • Vratas and Austerities — Devotees often undertake specific vows (vratas) for the entire month, such as chanting extra rounds of the Maha Mantra, reading more scriptures, or eating simple meals.
  • Visiting Holy Places — It is highly auspicious to reside in or visit Vrindavan or Mathura during this month, though wherever one observes the vrata with devotion becomes non-different from Vrindavan.

How We Celebrate at Math Mandir Chandigarh

Throughout the month of Kartik, the atmosphere at the Math is charged with deep devotion. The evening program is the highlight of the day. The temple is softly illuminated, and hundreds of devotees gather to offer individual ghee lamps to the deities while the sweet melody of the Damodarashtakam fills the air.

Special harikatha sessions are held focusing on the childhood pastimes of Krishna in Vrindavan and the glories of Srimati Radharani (as Kartik is also known as the month of Urja, or Radharani's month).

The month concludes with the grand festival of Govardhan Puja and Annakut, where a massive mountain of prasadam is offered to the Lord and then joyously distributed among the devotees.

Spiritual Significance

The Skanda Purana states that of all months, Kartik is the most dear to Lord Krishna. Any spiritual activity performed during this month yields immensely multiplied results.

The offering of a simple lamp is said to dispel the darkness of ignorance and lifetimes of accumulated karma, but more importantly, it awakens the dormant love for God in the heart of the practitioner. It is a time for inner reflection, purification, and deepening one's commitment to the path of devotion.
