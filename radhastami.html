Bhadra · September

Sri Radhastami

श्री राधाष्टमी — श्रीमती राधारानी का आविर्भाव दिवस

The divine appearance of the supreme goddess of devotion

The Story Behind the Festival

Fifteen days after the appearance of Lord Krishna on Janmashtami, on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the waxing moon in the month of Bhadra, Srimati Radharani appeared in the village of Barsana, near Mathura. She was found as a newborn on a golden lotus flower by King Vrishabhanu and his queen Kirtida.

The scriptures describe that She kept Her eyes closed for the first days of Her life, and opened them only when baby Krishna was brought before Her. The moment Their eyes met, it was the reunion of the eternal Divine Couple — the inseparable Shakti and Shaktiman, the energy and the energetic.

Srimati Radharani is considered the highest expression of divine love (mahabhava-svarupini). She is not merely a consort of Krishna, but the very embodiment of His internal pleasure potency (hladini-shakti). Without understanding Radharani, one cannot truly understand Krishna — for it is through Her love that His full glory is revealed.

"Among all the gopis, Srimati Radharani is the foremost. She surpasses all in beauty, in good qualities, in good fortune and, above all, in love." — Ujjvala-nilamani

Common Practices & Traditions

  • Fasting until noon — Devotees observe a half-day fast, breaking it at midday with a light meal before the main feast in the evening.
  • Special abhishek of Srimati Radharani — The deity of Radharani is bathed with rose water, saffron milk, and panchamrit, and adorned with fresh flower garlands.
  • Offering of Her favourite items — Sweets made from milk and sugar (especially laddus, peda, and kheer), fresh lotus flowers, and the colour pink/red feature prominently.
  • Reciting Her glories — Harikatha focuses on Radharani's transcendental qualities, Her pastimes in Vrindavan, and Her supreme position in the devotional tradition.
  • Singing Radha-bhajans — Songs composed by the great Vaishnava poets glorifying Radharani are sung with deep emotion and devotion.

How We Celebrate at Math Mandir Chandigarh

Radhastami at the Chandigarh Math is an intimate and deeply devotional celebration. The temple is decorated in shades of pink and gold, with fresh roses and jasmine adorning the altar. The deities of Sri Sri Radha Madhava are dressed in exquisite new garments.

Morning harikatha centres on the glories of Srimati Radharani as described by the Acharyas of the lineage. Devotees recite the names and attributes of Radharani — Her compassion, Her beauty, Her unparalleled love for Krishna, and Her role as the original guru of all devotees.

In the evening, a beautiful abhishek ceremony is performed specifically for Srimati Radharani's murti. Devotees offer Her favourite sweets, and a grand feast rich in milk-based preparations is served to all present.

Significance for Devotees

In the Gaudiya tradition, Srimati Radharani occupies the highest place. Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu Himself is understood to be Krishna who has taken the mood and complexion of Radharani — meaning that the entire Gaudiya mission flows from Her love. To worship Radharani is to access the most intimate path to Krishna.

The Acharyas teach that the mercy of Radharani is even more powerful than the mercy of Krishna Himself, because She never refuses a sincere seeker. Her compassion knows no bounds — and Radhastami is the day when that compassion flows most freely.
